# Texas Application Readiness

## Overview
The Office of the Attorney General (OAG) in Texas requires human trafficking training for employees in commercial lodging establishments. House Bill 390 mandates annual training for all employees with 10 or more rooms and all employees at smaller lodging establishments.

## Materials Needed
- Harbor Alliance must prepare a comprehensive human trafficking training program that covers:
  - Identifying signs of human trafficking
  - Responding to suspected incidents
  - Understanding anti-trafficking signage requirements

## Standards to Meet
- Training videos approved by the Office of the Attorney General (OAG)
- Minimum duration: Not specified, but should be effective and engaging
- Compliance with OAG guidelines for content and delivery methods

## Likely Review Risks/Gaps
1. **Training Content Accuracy**:
   - Ensure that the training materials are accurate and up-to-date.
   - Verify that they cover all required topics as outlined by the OAG.

2. **Employee Access to Training**:
   - Confirm that all employees (both those with 10+ rooms and smaller establishments) have access to the training program.
   - Schedule regular reminders for annual training completion.

3. **Documentation of Compliance**:
   - Maintain records of completed training sessions.
   - Provide proof of annual training completion to OAG upon request.

4. **Anti-Trafficking Signage**:
   - Verify that all required anti-trafficking signage is posted in visible locations within the lodging establishment.

5. **Employee Awareness and Reporting**:
   - Ensure that employees understand their roles in reporting suspected human trafficking incidents.
   - Establish a clear reporting mechanism within the workplace.

6. **Ongoing Compliance Review**:
   - Regularly review training programs for effectiveness.
   - Stay updated on any changes to OAG requirements or guidance.

## Conclusion
Harbor Alliance must thoroughly prepare and implement a human trafficking training program that meets all OAG requirements. This includes accurate content, accessible training materials, and proper documentation of compliance. By following these guidelines, Harbor Alliance can ensure readiness for the Texas OAG application submission.
