# Texas Application Readiness

## Overview

The Office of the Attorney General (OAG) in Texas requires commercial lodging establishments with 10 or more rooms to provide annual human trafficking training to their employees. The requirements include recognizing signs of trafficking, reporting suspected incidents, and understanding anti-trafficking signage.

## Materials Needed

1. **Human Trafficking Training Videos**:
   - Approved by the Office and accessible online or in-person at certain organizations.
   
2. **Anti-Trafficking Signage**:
   - Must be posted in all visible areas within the lodging establishment.
   - Sample signage provided by the OAG.

## Standards to Meet

1. **Annual Training Requirement**:
   - All employees of commercial lodging establishments with 10 or more rooms must complete annual training.
   - Employees at all lodging establishments regardless of size are encouraged but not required to complete the training.

2. **Training Content**:
   - The training must cover identifying signs of trafficking, reporting suspected incidents, and understanding anti-trafficking signage.

3. **Training Duration**:
   - At least 20 minutes of classroom or other effective interactive training.

4. **Employee Roles in Reporting**:
   - Employees must be equipped to report observed instances of human trafficking within their establishments.

## Likely Review Risks/Gaps

1. **Compliance Monitoring**:
   - Ongoing monitoring by the lodging establishment to ensure all employees receive and complete the required annual training.
   
2. **Signage Placement**:
   - Verification that anti-trafficking signage is visible in all areas of the lodging establishment.

3. **Employee Training Records**:
   - Keeping records of when and how each employee completed the training, including dates and certifications.

4. **Awareness Campaigns**:
   - Organizing regular awareness campaigns to reinforce training and ensure ongoing knowledge.

5. **Legal Penalties**:
   - Understanding potential legal penalties for non-compliance with OAG requirements.
   
6. **Training Effectiveness**:
   - Assessing the effectiveness of the training provided and making necessary adjustments.

## Next Steps

1. **Prepare Training Materials**:
   - Obtain approved human trafficking training videos and anti-trafficking signage from the OAG.

2. **Schedule Training Sessions**:
   - Plan and schedule annual training sessions for all eligible employees.

3. **Post Signage**:
   - Ensure that anti-trafficking signage is prominently displayed in all visible areas of the lodging establishment.

4. **Document Training Completion**:
   - Maintain records of each employee's completion of the training, including dates and certifications.

5. **Conduct Awareness Campaigns**:
   - Regularly review and reinforce human trafficking awareness within the workplace.

6. **Monitor Compliance**:
   - Regularly check compliance with OAG requirements to ensure ongoing readiness for potential audits or inspections.
