## Summary
Connecticut has comprehensive laws and training programs in place to address human trafficking, including sex and labor. The state requires annual basic training for all Child Protective Services staff, with advanced training options available. Training is also provided for foster parents, service providers, schools, law enforcement, emergency medical services (EMS), and other professionals, as well as specific curricula for youth and hotel/motel workers.

## Extracted Requirements
- **Applicable industry:** Child protective services, foster care, schools, law enforcement, EMS, hospitals, hospitality industry, and youth.
- **Training required:** Annual basic training for Child Protective Services staff, advanced training options available. Specific trainings for foster parents, service providers, schools, law enforcement, EMS, hospitals, hospitality industry, and youth.
- **Signage required:** Not explicitly mentioned in the sources provided.
- **Reporting required:** Hotels, motels, inns, and similar lodging must keep guest transactions and receipts for at least 6 months and report trafficking referrals.
- **Enforcement/penalties:** Laws include mandatory data reporting and a statewide oversight and monitoring body. Penalties vary from class C felonies to presumptions of coercion.

## Enforcement/Implementation
- **Training Implementation:** Training programs are mandated for various stakeholders, including Child Protective Services staff, foster parents, schools, law enforcement, EMS, hospitals, hospitality industry workers, and youth.
- **Legal Framework:** Multiple public acts have been enacted to strengthen protections for victims of human trafficking, expand the crime of human trafficking, and require training and reporting by hotels/motels.
