### Analysis of the Sources on Human Trafficking Training Requirements for Hotel and Motel Employees

**Sources:**
1. **SB 970 (external site)** - A California state law that requires hotel and motel employers to provide human trafficking awareness training to employees.
2. **Human Trafficking FAQs (linked from SOURCE 2)** - Offers information on the training requirements for hotel and motel employees as per SB 970.
3. **Model Notice for Human Trafficking | State of California** - Provides a template notice that businesses must post informing the public and victims of human trafficking about hotline numbers.

### Key Findings:

1. **Training Requirement:**
   - **SB 970 (external site)** states that by January 1, 2020, hotel and motel employers must provide at least 20 minutes of classroom or other effective interactive training regarding human trafficking awareness to each employee who is likely to interact or come into contact with victims of human trafficking.
   - **SOURCE 3** also confirms this requirement, noting that hotels, motels, and bed and breakfast inns are among the businesses required to post a model notice.

2. **Timing:**
   - Employees hired after July 1, 2019 must be trained within six months of their employment.
   - Employees who were already employed as of July 1, 2019, are not required to receive additional training if they have already received it before January 1, 2020.

3. **Penalties:**
   - Failure to comply with the training requirement can result in penalties under California law.

4. **Posting Requirement:**
   - Businesses must post a model notice informing the public and victims of human trafficking about hotline numbers. This notice must be posted in English and Spanish, and in certain counties, a third language as well (depending on federal Voting Rights Act provisions).

### Summary:
Hotel and motel employers are required by California law to provide at least 20 minutes of training regarding human trafficking awareness to their employees who are likely to interact with victims. This training must be completed within six months of employment for new hires, or before January 1, 2020, for existing employees. Non-compliance can result in penalties under California law.

**Note:** These requirements aim to protect vulnerable populations and ensure that employees are aware of the signs of human trafficking and know how to report suspicious activities.
