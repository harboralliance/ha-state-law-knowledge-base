The Texas Legislature passed House Bill 390, which requires the Office of the Attorney General (OAG) to approve training for the hospitality industry. This annual training requirement only applies to commercial lodging establishments with ten or more rooms but is encouraged for all lodging establishments in the state regardless of size.

To meet this requirement, establishments must ensure their employees receive annual human trafficking training and provide proof of completion. The approved training videos can be accessed through various organizations, including:

1. Office of the Attorney General: "Human Trafficking Hotel Training" (22 minutes, English, free, online).
2. Etactics: "Human Trafficking – Texas" (26 minutes, English, $1/user/month, online).
3. Unbound Now: "Identifying and Responding to Human Trafficking: Commercial Lodging & Hospitality Industry" (25 minutes, English, free, both online and in-person).
4. Businesses Ending Slavery and Trafficking: Inhospitable to Human Trafficking Training (45-60 minutes, English, $25.95, both online and in-person).
5. Extended Stay America: "Human Trafficking Training" (30 minutes, English, free, available via internal website only accessible to ESA associates).
6. Marriott International: "Recognize and Respond: Addressing Human Trafficking in the Hospitality Industry" (60 or 80 minutes, English, free, available to Marriott associates).
7. ECPAT-USA: "Your Role in Preventing Human Trafficking: Recognize the Signs" (30 minutes, English and Spanish*).
8. Refugee Services of Texas: "Survivor Services/HT101" (60 minutes, English, free, in-person).
9. PBJ Learning: Human Trafficking Essentials: Texas Hospitality Awareness HB 390 (26 minutes, English, $24.99, online).
10. Compliance Learning Solutions: Combating Human Trafficking in the Hospitality Industry (45 minutes, English, $25.95, online).

The training videos cover various aspects of human trafficking and provide guidance on identifying potential victims and responding appropriately. Employers must ensure their employees receive this training to comply with the requirements set forth by House Bill 390.
