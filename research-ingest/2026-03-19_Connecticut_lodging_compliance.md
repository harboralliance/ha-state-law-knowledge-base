## Summary
Connecticut has comprehensive laws and training programs to address human trafficking. The state requires annual training for DCF staff, foster parents, and other service providers on identifying and responding to human trafficking cases. Legislation includes expanding protections for victims, establishing multidisciplinary teams, and requiring hotels and motels to provide trafficking training annually.

## Extracted Requirements
- **Applicable industry:** Hotels, motels, inns, similar lodging; DCF staff; foster parents; service providers; schools; law enforcement; hospital and medical staff; hospitality industry; other professionals.
- **Training required:** Annual human trafficking training for DCF staff, foster parents, and service providers; specialized trainings for emergency medical services personnel, teachers, and police officers.
- **Signage required:** Hotels, motels, inns, similar lodging must provide trafficking training and awareness campaigns to all employees annually and keep guest transactions and receipts for at least 6 months.
- **Reporting required:** State attorneys and local law enforcement must document training, investigations of missing children, trafficking referrals, trafficking cases referred for prosecution, etc.
- **Enforcement measures:** Hotels, motels, inns, similar lodging must provide trafficking training annually and keep guest transactions and receipts for at least 6 months.
- **Legislation enacted:** Public Acts addressing human trafficking from 2017 to 2015.

## Enforcement Measures
- **Training:** Annual human trafficking training for key personnel such as DCF staff, foster parents, service providers, teachers, police officers, and hotel staff.
- **Documentation:** State attorneys and local law enforcement must document training, investigations, referrals, and prosecutions related to human trafficking.
- **Data Reporting:** Mandatory data reporting to ensure ongoing monitoring of efforts to combat trafficking.

## Legislation
Connecticut has enacted several laws aimed at strengthening protections for victims of human trafficking, expanding the crime of human trafficking, and requiring hotels and motels to provide annual trafficking training. These laws also include provisions for victim services, multidisciplinary teams, and expanded conditions for legal proceedings.

### Summary of Laws
1. **2017 Public Act 17-32:** Expanded human trafficking crimes and provided enhanced protections for victims.
2. **2016 Public Act 16-71:** Defined commercial sexual exploitation and increased penalties.
3. **2015 Public Act 15-195:** Strengthened victim protections, expanded conditions for erasure of records, and authorized wiretapping.
4. **2014 Public Act 14-186:** Provided training to law enforcement on human trafficking and created multidisciplinary teams.
5. **2013 Public Act 13-166:** Aligned with federal TVPA, expanding penalties for sexual exploitation and trafficking.
6. **2012 Public Act 12-141:** Defined commercial sexual exploitation of a minor as a class C felony.
7. **2011 Public Act 11-180:** Required notification by DCF when youth are arrested for prostitution and provided out-of-state placements.
8. **2010 Public Act 10-115:** Provided a safe harbor for exploited children.

## Conclusion
Connecticut's comprehensive approach to combating human trafficking includes robust legislation, mandatory training programs, and enforcement measures to ensure that victims receive the necessary support while perpetrators are held accountable.
