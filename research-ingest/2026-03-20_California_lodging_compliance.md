### Summary of Key Information from Sources

#### **1. Training Requirement for Hotel and Motel Employees**
   - **Source**: SB 970, enacted in California.
   - **Requirement**: By January 1, 2020, hotel and motel employers must provide at least 20 minutes of classroom or other effective interactive training regarding human trafficking awareness to employees who interact with victims of trafficking. New employees should receive this training within six months of employment.
   - **Exception**: Employers who provided the training before January 1, 2019, are not required to provide additional training.

#### **2. Posting Requirement for Human Trafficking Hotline Numbers**
   - **Source**: Senate Bill 1193, enacted in California.
   - **Requirement**: Hotels, motels, and bed and breakfast inns must post a notice informing the public and victims of human trafficking about the telephone hotline numbers available for help or reporting unlawful activity. This requirement applies as of April 1, 2013, and was further clarified by SB 225 and AB 260 (2017).
   - **Languages**: The notice must be posted in English and Spanish, with additional languages required in specific counties.

#### **3. Enforcement and Penalties**
   - **Non-compliance**: Failure to post the notice or provide required training can result in penalties.
   - **Posting Mandates**: Each mandated business is required to post the notice in English and Spanish. For businesses in specific counties, a third language posting may be necessary.

#### **4. Model Notice for Human Trafficking**
   - **Source**: California Attorney General's Office.
   - **Model Notice**: A model notice for human trafficking can be downloaded from the Office of the Attorney General website. This notice must include telephone hotline numbers to seek help or report unlawful activity and, optionally, a text number for services and support.

#### **Training Requirement**
   - **Who**: Hotel and motel employers
   - **What**: Training on human trafficking awareness for employees who interact with victims
   - **When**: By January 1, 2020 (original deadline), with a six-month extension for new hires
   - **Exemption**: Employers who provided the training before January 1, 2019, are exempt from providing additional training

#### **Posting Requirement**
   - **Who**: Hotels, motels, and bed and breakfast inns
   - **What**: Posting a notice informing the public about human trafficking hotline numbers
   - **When**: As of April 1, 2013 (original deadline), further clarified by SB 225 and AB 260 (2017)
   - **Languages**: English, Spanish, and additional languages required in specific counties

#### **Enforcement**
   - **Penalties**: Non-compliance with posting or training requirements can result in penalties.
   - **Post Requirements**: Notice must be posted in multiple languages as specified by California law.

#### **Model Notice**
   - **What**: A standardized notice template for human trafficking that includes hotline numbers and, optionally, a text number
   - **Where to Obtain**: Available on the California Attorney General's website

### Conclusion

The training requirement and posting of notices are key provisions designed to increase awareness of human trafficking among hotel and motel employees and the public. The model notice serves as a standardized reference for compliance with these requirements. Failure to comply can result in penalties, emphasizing the importance of adherence to these regulations.
