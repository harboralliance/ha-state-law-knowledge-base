The key provisions of this bill are:

1. Requires employers at lodging establishments, restaurants and truck stops to provide human trafficking recognition training for employees who have recurring public interactions.

2. Training must be completed within 6 months of starting the job role and every 2 years thereafter if still employed in that role.

3. Training must be at least 20 minutes long and cover:
   - Definition of human trafficking
   - Identification of individuals at risk for trafficking 
   - Difference between labor vs sex trafficking relevant to lodging/food/truck stop employees
   - Role of employees in reporting and responding to trafficking

4. Employers can use their own training program or third party programs that meet these requirements.

5. The state Department must develop a model curriculum if an employer does not administer its own.

6. For violations, local governments/law enforcement can issue a notice of non-compliance with a 30 day cure period before fines up to $1,500 per offense.

7. The bill becomes effective January 1, 2026.

The goal is to educate employees and employers about human trafficking risks and proper responses at these types of establishments that have frequent public interactions.
