### Summary of House Bill 2026

**Title**: Human Trafficking Awareness and Prevention Act

**Purpose**: This bill aims to enhance awareness and prevention of human trafficking within lodging establishments, restaurants, and truck stops. It mandates that these establishments provide their employees with training on recognizing human trafficking and reporting it.

**Key Provisions**:

1. **Definitions**:
   - "Department": Department of Human Services.
   - "Employee": A person employed by a lodging establishment who has recurring interactions with the public (e.g., receptionists, housekeepers).
   - "Employer": The owner or operator of a lodging establishment, restaurant, or truck stop.
   - "Human Trafficking": Deprivation or violation of personal liberty for forced labor, commercial sex, or other exploitation.

2. **Training Requirements**:
   - Beginning June 1, 2020, employers must provide their employees with training on recognizing human trafficking and reporting it. The training must be completed within 6 months of employment and every 2 years thereafter.
   - Training must be at least 20 minutes in duration.

3. **Curriculum**:
   - Employers may use their own program or third-party programs that meet the minimum criteria set by the Department, including definitions of human trafficking, risk factors, differences between labor and sex trafficking, and roles of employees in reporting incidents.
   - The Department must develop a standardized curriculum for establishments not administering their own training. This includes a definition of human trafficking, risk identification, distinctions between labor and sex trafficking, and employee roles.

4. **Penalties**:
   - Local government units or law enforcement agencies may monitor compliance and issue reasonable notices to employers.
   - If a violation is not corrected within 30 days, the Attorney General or State's Attorney can bring a civil action against the employer.
   - An offense under this act results in a business offense with fines up to $1,500 per offense.

5. **Effective Date**:
   - The bill takes effect January 1, 2026.

### Impact

- **Enhanced Awareness**: By mandating training, the bill aims to increase awareness of human trafficking among employees who have frequent public interactions.
- **Improved Reporting**: Training will help employees recognize potential signs of human trafficking and report them appropriately, potentially leading to earlier interventions and protection of victims.
- **Standardization**: The Department's curriculum ensures a consistent approach across different establishments, providing a standardized framework for training.
- **Legal Protection**: The penalties outlined in the bill serve as a deterrent against non-compliance and provide legal recourse if employers fail to meet their obligations.

### Conclusion

House Bill 2026 represents an important step towards combating human trafficking by educating employees who have frequent public interactions about the signs of human trafficking and encouraging them to report potential incidents. The implementation of this bill could lead to a reduction in human trafficking within hospitality and transportation industries, thereby protecting vulnerable individuals.
