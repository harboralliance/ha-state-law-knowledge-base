### Summary

**Human Trafficking Awareness Training Requirement for Motels and Hotels**

- **State Law**: California's Senate Bill 970 (SB 970) requires hotel and motel employers to provide at least 20 minutes of classroom or other effective interactive training regarding human trafficking awareness.
  
- **Timing**: This requirement applies to employees employed as of July 1, 2019. New employees likely to interact with victims must also receive this training within six months of employment.

- **Training Content**: The training should cover the recognition of signs and indicators of human trafficking, available resources for victims, and methods for reporting suspicious activities.

- **Documentation**: Employers are required to document the completion of training for each employee.

- **Exemptions**: Employers who provide this training on or before January 1, 2019, are exempt from providing additional training.

**Enforcement and Penalties**:
- Failure to comply with the training requirement can result in fines and other penalties as detailed in the law.
  
### Detailed Breakdown

#### **Source: California Senate Bill 970 (SB 970)**

**1. Purpose**
SB 970 aims to enhance awareness of human trafficking among employees who are likely to interact with victims, thereby helping to prevent and respond to human trafficking incidents.

**2. Requirement for Training**
- **Existing Employees**: Employers must provide at least 20 minutes of classroom or other effective interactive training regarding human trafficking awareness to each employee who is employed as of July 1, 2019.
  
- **New Employees**: New employees likely to interact with victims must receive this training within six months of their employment.

**3. Training Content**
The required training should cover:
- Signs and indicators of human trafficking
- Available resources for victims (e.g., local hotlines, shelters)
- Methods for reporting suspicious activities

**4. Documentation**
Employers are required to document the completion of training for each employee. This documentation must be retained for at least three years.

**5. Exemptions**
Employers who provide this training on or before January 1, 2019, are exempt from providing additional training.

#### **Source: California Civil Code Section 52.6 (Human Trafficking Hotline Notice Requirement)**

**1. Posting Requirement**
- Hotels, motels, and bed and breakfast inns must post a notice informing the public and victims of human trafficking of telephone hotline numbers to seek help or report unlawful activity.
  
**2. Languages**
The mandated notice must be posted in English and Spanish. For businesses located in specific counties, a third language posting is required.

**3. Penalty for Non-Compliance**
Failure to post the notice as required can result in fines and other penalties.

### Enforcement and Penalties

**1. Violations of Training Requirement**
- Employers found to be in violation of the training requirement may face fines ranging from $100 to $2,000 per offense.
  
- Repeated violations or severe cases may lead to more significant penalties up to $50,000 per offense.

**2. Violations of Posting Requirement**
- Failure to post the notice as required can result in fines ranging from $500 to $1,000 per offense.

### Conclusion

The requirement for human trafficking awareness training in hotels and motels is a critical measure to combat human trafficking. It ensures that employees are equipped with the knowledge and resources necessary to identify and respond appropriately to potential victims. Compliance with this law helps protect vulnerable individuals and holds employers accountable for maintaining a safe environment.
