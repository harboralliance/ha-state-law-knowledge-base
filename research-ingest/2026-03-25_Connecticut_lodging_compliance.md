## Summary
Connecticut has comprehensive legislation and training programs aimed at combating human trafficking, including both sex and labor trafficking. The state requires mandatory annual training for foster parents, Child Protective Services staff, law enforcement, hotels/motels, and other professionals. Legislation includes provisions to expand protections for victims, allow the erasure of records for minors, and provide specific laws against commercial sexual exploitation.

## Extracted Requirements
- **Applicable industry**: Foster care services, Child Protective Services, Law enforcement, Hotels/Motels, Schools, Hospitality industry, Medical providers
- **Training required**: Annual training for foster parents, Child Protective Services staff, law enforcement (POST Certified), hotels/motels, schools, and hospitality industry professionals. Two-day training program for providers.
- **Signage required**: Not specified in the provided sources.
- **Reporting required**: State attorneys and local law enforcement must document training, investigations of missing children, trafficking referrals, and cases referred for prosecution.
- **Enforcement agency**: Department of Public Health (DPH), Office of Victim Services (OVS)
- **Penalties**: Class C felony for knowingly purchasing advertising space for commercial sex acts involving minors.

## Legislation Overview
- **Public Act 17-32 (2017)**: Expands conditions under which human trafficking can be committed when the victim is a minor.
- **Public Act 16-71 (2016)**: Defines prostitution and expands requirements for documenting training, investigations, and trafficking cases.
- **Public Act 15-195 (2015)**: Expands protections for victims of human trafficking, including services for sexual assault victims.
- **Public Act 14-186 (2014)**: Provides services to uncared-for human trafficking victims and requires DCF training on human trafficking.
- **Public Act 13-166 (2013)**: Aligns with federal law, making commercial sexual exploitation of a minor a class C felony.
- **Public Act 12-141 (2012)**: Defines commercial sexual exploitation of a minor as a class C felony.
- **Public Act 11-180 (2011)**: Requires DCF notification upon arrest for prostitution.
- **Public Act 10-115 (2010)**: Provides a safe harbor for exploited children.

## Training Programs
Connecticut offers various training programs tailored to different industries and professionals:
- **Foster parents**: Annual mandatory training.
- **Child Protective Services staff**: Annual mandatory training.
- **Law enforcement (POST Certified)**: Two-day training program.
- **Hotels/Motels, Schools, Hospitality industry professionals**: Annual training.

These requirements ensure that all relevant personnel in Connecticut are equipped with the necessary knowledge and resources to identify, prevent, and respond to human trafficking incidents effectively.
