The provided text is a draft of an Illinois state law that aims to prevent human trafficking and protect vulnerable individuals within lodging establishments, restaurants, and truck stops. Here are the key points of this bill:

### Key Definitions:
- **Department**: The Department of Human Services.
- **Employee**: A person employed by a lodging establishment, restaurant, or truck stop who interacts with the public regularly.
- **Employer**: A person or entity operating a lodging establishment, restaurant, or truck stop.
- **Human Trafficking**: The deprivation or violation of personal liberty to obtain forced labor or services, procure or sell individuals for commercial sex, or exploit them in obscene matter.

### Training Requirements:
- Starting June 1, 2020, all employers (lodging establishments, restaurants, and truck stops) must provide their employees with training on recognizing human trafficking and the protocols for reporting such incidents. 
- Employees are required to complete this training within 6 months of starting their role and every 2 years thereafter if they remain in the position.
- The training must be at least 20 minutes long.

### Training Curriculum:
- Employers can use their own programs or third-party programs as long as they meet certain minimum requirements, which include:
  - Defining human trafficking and commercial exploitation of children.
  - Guidance on identifying individuals at risk for human trafficking.
  - Differentiating between labor and sex-based trafficking in the context of lodging establishments, restaurants, and truck stops.
  - Guidance on employees' roles in reporting and responding to incidents of human trafficking.

### Penalties:
- Local governments or law enforcement agencies can monitor compliance with this act.
- If a violation is discovered within 30 days of notice, employers may face civil penalties.
- The Attorney General or State's Attorney can bring a civil action if the violation persists after the cure period.

### Effective Date:
This law takes effect on January 1, 2026.

This legislation aims to enhance awareness and preparedness among employees in hospitality and transportation sectors to identify and respond to signs of human trafficking, thereby providing a safer environment for vulnerable individuals.
