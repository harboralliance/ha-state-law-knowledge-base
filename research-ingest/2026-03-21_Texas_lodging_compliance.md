### Overview of Texas Human Trafficking Laws and Training Requirements

#### **House Bill 390 (87th Regular Session)**
- **Purpose**: Requires the Office of the Attorney General to approve training for workers directly employed by certain commercial lodging establishments to prevent human trafficking.
- **Scope**: Applies only to commercial lodging establishments with 10 or more rooms but is encouraged for all lodging establishments in Texas.

#### **Training Requirements:**
- **Annual Training**: Employees must complete annual human trafficking training.
- **Initial Training**: New employees must complete the training within 90 days of hire.
- **Certification**: Approved training providers must provide a certificate of completion to every employee who completes the training, including the date taken and course title.
- **Documentation**: Employers must retain records of completed training certificates and make them available for review by the Office of the Attorney General.

### Training Content

#### **Key Components of the Training:**
1. **Identification of Red Flags**: Specific indicators that an individual may be at risk of human trafficking.
2. **Role in Reporting**: Guidance on how employees can report suspected human trafficking and contact appropriate entities such as the National Human Trafficking Hotline, local law enforcement, and others.
3. **Signage Requirements**: Information on the required human trafficking signage to be displayed in commercial lodging establishments.
4. **Employee Protections**: Emphasis that employees may not be disciplined or retaliated against for making good-faith reports of suspected human trafficking.
