The text provided appears to be an amended version of a statute (likely Illinois General Assembly Bill 101) regarding human trafficking recognition training for employees in lodging establishments, restaurants, and truck stops. The key points are:

- Employers must provide annual human trafficking recognition training for their employees who interact with the public.
- Training must include definitions of human trafficking, identifying at-risk individuals, distinguishing labor vs sex trafficking relevant to these industries, and guidance on reporting and responding to incidents.
- There's an option for employers to use their own program or a third-party one that meets minimum requirements.
- The Department of Human Services is tasked with developing a model curriculum that will be used if an employer does not develop its own.
- Violations can result in enforcement by local government agencies or the filing of a civil action, subject to fines up to $1,500 per offense.

The key difference from the previous version (P.A. 101-18) is the inclusion of Section 20, which establishes penalties for non-compliance with the new requirements. This makes it enforceable as part of state law rather than just a regulation or guideline.
