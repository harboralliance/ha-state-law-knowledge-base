The content provided includes regulations and guidelines for hotels, motels, and short-term rentals in Minnesota regarding sex trafficking prevention and response training.

Key Points:
1. Training Requirements:
   - All hotel owners, managers, and on-site employees must complete the required training within 90 days of hire.
   - All required staff must retake this training every year.
   - Employees under 16 (unless they clean rooms), those working exclusively in restaurants providing catering services, or those with no direct contact with guests are exempt.

2. Training Package:
   - The package includes a virtual presentation, a Training Companion Guide, and Manager & Owner Training Toolkit.

3. Display of Posters:
   - All required staff must display the back-of-house poster in a location all staff will see.

4. Ongoing Awareness Campaign:
   - Hotels, motels, and short-term rentals need to conduct an ongoing awareness campaign for employees.

5. Regulatory Compliance:
   - The law applies only to those holding hotel licenses, not resorts, bed-and-breakfasts, vacation shares, or short-term rentals unless they also hold a hotel license.
   
6. Training Delivery:
   - Training can be accessed through MDH Learning Center in English and Spanish with plans to add Hmong and Somali in the future.

7. Frequency of Training:
   - The training must be completed annually to comply with the law.

8. Exemptions:
   - Employees under 16, those exclusively working in restaurants or bars, and those with no direct contact with guests are exempt.

9. Licensing Verification:
   - Hotels should verify their licensing status as per MDH guidelines.

10. Non-Replacement of Training Materials:
    - Only parts of the training package can be replaced with prior review and approval from MDH.

This comprehensive approach aims to educate staff in the hospitality industry about recognizing and responding to instances of sex trafficking, thereby helping to prevent and mitigate its impact within their establishments.
