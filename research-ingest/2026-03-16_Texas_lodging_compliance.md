Based on the provided sources, here is an overview of human trafficking training requirements and resources for commercial lodging establishments in Texas:

1. Training Requirement:
   - Passed by House Bill 390 in the 87th Regular Session.
   - Applies to commercial lodging establishments with 10 or more rooms.
   - Encouraged but not required for smaller establishments.

2. Key Components of Required Training:
   - Identification of human trafficking risk factors
   - Role of staff in reporting and responding to human trafficking incidents
   - Information on the National Human Trafficking Hotline and local law enforcement agencies
   - Understanding of commercial lodging's role in addressing sex trafficking
   - Information on human trafficking signage required

3. Training Frequency:
   - New employees must complete training within 90 days of hire.
   - All employees must receive annual training.

4. Certificate Requirement:
   - Approved training providers must provide a certificate of completion to each employee.
   - Operators must retain copies of certificates for three years after issuance.

5. Training Approvals:
   - Courses must be approved by the Office of the Attorney General (OAG).
   - The OAG maintains an approved course list and approves new courses as they are developed.

6. Approved Training Resources:
   - List includes various video training programs from organizations like Etactics, Unbound Now, Inhospitable to Human Trafficking, Marriott International, ECPAT-USA, Refugee Services of Texas, PBJ Learning, and Compliance Learning Solutions.
   - Courses cover topics such as identifying human trafficking, reporting incidents, and understanding the hospitality industry's role in addressing human trafficking.

7. Costs:
   - Some courses are free, while others may require a one-time fee or monthly subscription.

8. Delivery Methods:
   - Online
   - In-person

9. Language Availability:
   - English and Spanish (for some courses)

10. Contact Information:
    - For course-specific questions, contact the training organization directly.
    - For general inquiries, contact the OAG at [email protected]

This comprehensive approach aims to educate commercial lodging staff about human trafficking risks, empower them to report incidents effectively, and provide them with the necessary information to contribute to the prevention of human trafficking in the hospitality industry.
