The document outlines a new law in Illinois regarding human trafficking recognition training for employees working at lodging establishments, restaurants, and truck stops. The key points are:

1. Training Requirement:
   - Employers must provide human trafficking recognition training to their employees who interact with the public.
   - Training is mandatory starting June 1, 2020, for new employees in this role and every 2 years thereafter for existing employees.

2. Training Content:
   - The training program must include:
     a) Definition of human trafficking
     b) Guidance on identifying individuals at risk for trafficking
     c) Differences between labor and sex trafficking relevant to the workplace
     d) Roles of employees in reporting and responding to trafficking

3. Curricula:
   - Employers can use their own training program or a third-party one that meets these requirements.
   - The Department of Human Services is tasked with developing a model curriculum.

4. Penalties for Non-Compliance:
   - Local governments or law enforcement agencies may monitor compliance and issue reasonable notices.
   - If the violation isn't corrected, they can bring a civil action against the employer.
   - Violators face fines up to $1,500 per offense.

5. Effective Date: 
   - The law takes effect on January 1, 2026.

This legislation aims to increase awareness and preparedness among hospitality industry workers regarding human trafficking, potentially helping to identify and respond to potential victims more effectively.
