# State Research Capture

Connecticut

## Summary
Connecticut has robust legislation and training programs in place to address human trafficking, both for sex and labor. The state requires mandatory annual trafficking training for foster parents, service providers, schools, law enforcement, hospital and medical staff, hospitality industry professionals, and other relevant sectors.

## Extracted Requirements
- **Applicable Industry:** Foster parents, service providers, schools, law enforcement, hospital and medical staff, hospitality industry professionals, and other relevant sectors.
- **Training Required:** Annual trafficking training for foster parents, service providers, schools, law enforcement (POST certified), hospitals, and hospitality industry.
- **Signage Required:** Hotels, motels, inns, and similar lodging must provide trafficking training and awareness campaigns to all employees annually. This includes signage at entrances or websites.
- **Reporting Required:** State attorneys and local law enforcement must document training, investigations of missing children, trafficking referrals, trafficking cases referred for prosecution, etc.
- **Enforcement Agency:** Department of Children and Families (DCF), Department of Public Health (DPH), Office of Victim Services (OVS).
- **Penalties/Legislation:** Public Acts such as 17-32, 16-71, 15-195, etc., expanding the crime of human trafficking, providing services for victims, and strengthening protections.
- **Public Awareness:** Ongoing monitoring of efforts to combat trafficking, mandatory data reporting, public awareness campaigns.

## Effective Policies and Initiatives
Connecticut's comprehensive approach includes:
1. **Legislation:** Expanding the definition of human trafficking, providing specific penalties, and establishing victim services.
2. **Training Programs:** Mandated annual training for various sectors to ensure understanding and proactive response.
3. **Data Reporting:** Compelling state agencies to document and report on trafficking cases and interventions.
4. **Public Awareness:** Implementing campaigns to educate the public about human trafficking and encourage reporting.

These policies and initiatives demonstrate Connecticut's commitment to combating human trafficking and providing support for victims.
