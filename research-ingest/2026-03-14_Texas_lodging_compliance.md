### Summary of Human Trafficking Training Requirements for Commercial Lodging Establishments in Texas

#### Overview
The Texas Legislature passed House Bill 390, which mandates human trafficking training for employees at commercial lodging establishments with 10 or more rooms. This requirement is aimed at increasing awareness and enabling workers to recognize and report potential instances of human trafficking.

### Training Requirements
1. **Employees of Commercial Lodging Establishments**:
   - Required to complete annual human trafficking training.
   - Employees must not be disciplined, retaliated against, or discriminated against for making a good faith report of suspected human trafficking.

2. **Timing and Frequency**:
   - New employees must complete the training within 90 days of hiring.
   - All employees must receive annual training.

3. **Training Content**:
   - Must cover identifying individuals at risk of human trafficking.
   - Must include information on reporting mechanisms (e.g., National Human Trafficking Hotline, local law enforcement).
   - Must inform on the requirements for displaying anti-human trafficking signage in commercial lodging establishments.
