The Texas Legislature passed House Bill 390, which requires the Office of the Attorney General (OAG) to approve training for the hospitality industry. This annual training requirement only applies to commercial lodging establishments with 10 or more rooms but is encouraged for all lodging establishments in the state, regardless of size.

### Key Requirements:
- **Annual Training**: Employees must receive training annually.
- **Training Content**: Training must cover identifying and responding to human trafficking in the hospitality industry.
- **Signage Requirement**: Commercial lodging establishments must display human trafficking signage.
- **Non-Discrimination**: Employees may not be disciplined, retaliated against, or discriminated against for making a good faith report of suspected human trafficking.
