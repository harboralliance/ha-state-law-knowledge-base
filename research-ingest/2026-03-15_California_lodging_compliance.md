## Overview of Human Trafficking Laws and Requirements for Motel and Hotel Employees

### Summary:
Human trafficking is a significant global issue, affecting millions of people worldwide. In response to this problem, various jurisdictions have enacted laws requiring businesses to provide training to their employees about human trafficking awareness. California, through Senate Bill 970 (SB 970), mandates that hotel and motel employers provide specific training to their staff regarding human trafficking.

### Key Points:

#### 1. **Legislation:**
   - **Senate Bill 970 (SB 970)**: This California law requires hotel and motel employers to provide at least 20 minutes of classroom or other effective interactive training and education regarding human trafficking awareness.
     - Effective Date: January 1, 2020
     - Training Requirement:
       - Employees likely to interact with victims must be trained as of July 1, 2019.
       - New employees in similar roles must receive training within six months of their employment.

#### 2. **Training Content:**
   - The training must cover the following aspects of human trafficking:
     - Identification and characteristics of human trafficking
     - Legal rights and resources available to victims
     - Prevention strategies and reporting procedures

#### 3. **Exceptions:**
   - Employers who have provided the required training on or before January 1, 2019, are not required to provide additional training.

### Implementation Details:

#### 4. **Training Delivery:**
   - Training can be conducted in various formats including classroom sessions, webinars, or self-paced online courses.
   - Employers must ensure that the training is effective and interactive to enhance employee understanding and retention of key information.

#### 5. **Documentation:**
   - Employers are required to maintain records of the training provided, including dates, types of training, and signatures of participants.
   - These records must be maintained for at least five years after the last date of training.

### Enforcement and Penalties:

#### 6. **Enforcement:**
   - The California Labor Commissioner enforces compliance with this law through inspections and investigations.
   - Non-compliance may result in penalties, including fines and potential citations.

#### 7. **Civil Remedies for Survivors:**
   - Victims of human trafficking may seek civil remedies under state law, which can include damages and other forms of compensation.

### Resources:

For further information on implementing SB 970, training materials, and resources to support employees, refer to the following:

- **California Department of Justice (DOJ)**: Offers model notices and guidance on compliance with human trafficking laws.
- **National Human Trafficking Resource Center**: Provides hotline numbers for reporting suspected cases of human trafficking.

### Conclusion:
The enforcement of SB 970 ensures that hotel and motel staff are better equipped to identify, respond to, and prevent human trafficking. By providing mandatory training, employers play a crucial role in safeguarding their employees and the public against this form of modern-day slavery.
