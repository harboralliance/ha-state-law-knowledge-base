### Overview

The laws and regulations regarding human trafficking in California are enforced by various state agencies, including the Department of Justice (DOJ), the Office of the Attorney General (OAG), and the Civil Code. These entities provide model notices and resources to ensure compliance with federal and state laws concerning human trafficking.

### Key Points from the Sources:

1. **SB 970 Requirement**:
   - Effective January 1, 2020.
   - Motel and hotel employers must provide at least 20 minutes of classroom or other effective interactive training regarding human trafficking awareness to employees who interact with victims.
   - Training is also required for new employees within six months of their hire.
   
2. **Human Trafficking Model Notice**:
   - The OAG provides a model notice that businesses (including hotels and motels) are required to post, which includes hotline numbers for seeking help or reporting unlawful activity.
   - These notices must be posted in English and Spanish, and in certain counties, an additional language may be required.

3. **Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
   - Failure to comply with posting requirements can result in fines and penalties as outlined in specific state laws such as SB 225 and AB 260 (2017).

4. **Resources for Support**:
   - Victims of human trafficking are encouraged to contact various hotlines, including the National Human Trafficking Resource Center at 888-373-7888 or TEXT: Be Free.
   
### Summary

The California State Government requires hotels and motels to provide specific training regarding human trafficking awareness and ensures businesses post model notices in compliance with federal and state laws. Violations of these requirements can lead to penalties, while victims are encouraged to seek help through designated hotlines.

These measures aim to protect victims of human trafficking and promote public awareness about the issue within the hospitality industry.
