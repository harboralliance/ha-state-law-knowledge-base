### Analysis and Summary

The provided information outlines requirements for human trafficking awareness training in hotels and motels, as well as the posting of model notices to inform the public about human trafficking resources. Below is a detailed summary based on the sources:

#### 1. **Human Trafficking Awareness Training**

- **California State Law (SB 970):** Effective from January 1, 2020.
  - **Training Requirement:**
    - Hotels and motels must provide at least 20 minutes of classroom or other effective interactive training on human trafficking awareness to employees who are likely to interact with victims of human trafficking.
    - The initial training is required for employees hired as of July 1, 2019, and for new hires within six months of their employment in roles where they might come into contact with trafficking victims.
  - **Exemption:**
    - Employers who have provided this training on or before January 1, 2019, are not required to provide additional training.

#### 2. **Model Notice for Posting**

- **Purpose:** To inform the public and victims of human trafficking about available resources, including telephone hotline numbers.
- **Posting Requirements:**
  - Businesses must post the notice in English and Spanish.
  - For businesses located in specific counties that are subject to federal Voting Rights Act language assistance provisions, a third language posting is required (e.g., Arabic, Chinese, Haitian Creole, etc.).
- **Resources:**
  - The mandated model notice can be downloaded from the website of the California Department of Justice.
  - Language-specific notices in multiple languages are available for download.

### Compliance and Enforcement

The law includes specific posting mandates, language requirements, and penalties for failure to post. Violations may result in fines or other legal consequences as outlined by the California Attorney General's Office and the Department of Justice.

### Key Points

- **Initial Training Deadline:** January 1, 2020.
- **Recurring Training Requirement:** For new hires within six months of their employment if they will interact with trafficking victims.
- **Exemption for Initial Compliance:** Employers who trained their staff before January 1, 2019, are not required to repeat the training.

### Conclusion

The California law on human trafficking awareness training and posting model notices is designed to ensure that employees in hotels and motels are informed about human trafficking and can provide assistance. This initiative aims to protect vulnerable individuals and promote public safety. Compliance with these requirements helps maintain a culture of awareness and support within the hospitality industry.
