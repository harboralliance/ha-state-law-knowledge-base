# State Research Capture

## State
Connecticut

## Summary
Connecticut provides various trainings for providers, foster parents, and communities on human trafficking to educate them about identifying, responding, and preventing trafficking. The state also has enacted multiple public acts addressing human trafficking, including sex trafficking, with penalties ranging from fines to imprisonment.

## Extracted Requirements
- **Applicable industry:** Child protective services, foster parents, service providers, schools, law enforcement, hospital and medical staff, hospitality industry, other professionals, youth, and the community.
- **Training required:** Introduction to Child Trafficking in Connecticut, Introduction to Human Trafficking in Connecticut for Law Enforcement - Police Officer's Standards and Training (POST) Certified, Child Trafficking of Boys, Trafficking of LGBQ/GNCT Children, Introduction to Labor Trafficking in Connecticut, Introduction to Labor Trafficking in Connecticut for Law Enforcement (POST Certified).
- **Signage required:** Not explicitly stated.
- **Reporting required:** Hotel, motel, and lodging businesses must keep guest transactions and receipts for at least 6 months. State attorneys and local law enforcement must document training, investigations of missing children, trafficking referrals, trafficking cases referred for prosecution, etc.
- **Enforcement:** Law enforcement agencies are required to document training and track investigations related to human trafficking.
- **Public awareness and prevention:** Ongoing monitoring of efforts to combat trafficking, mandatory data reporting, statewide oversight and monitoring body.

## Enforcement
Connecticut has enacted several public acts aimed at strengthening protections for victims of human trafficking. These include laws that expand the crime of human trafficking, require hotels and motels to provide trafficking training annually, and mandate the erasure of juvenile records in certain circumstances. The state also provides services to human trafficking victims classified as "uncared for" and requires DCF to provide training to law enforcement on human trafficking of minors.

## Summary
Connecticut's approach to combating human trafficking involves comprehensive legislation, mandatory trainings, and monitoring efforts across various industries and sectors.
