Based on the text provided, here are the key points:

1. This is an amendment (P.A. 102-324) to an existing law that requires training for employees at lodging establishments, restaurants, and truck stops.

2. The new requirements go into effect on June 1, 2020.

3. Employees must receive training in recognizing human trafficking and reporting it to the appropriate authorities. 

4. Training must be completed within 6 months of starting a job at one of these establishments, and every 2 years thereafter if still employed there.

5. The training should be at least 20 minutes long and cover:
   - Definition of human trafficking
   - Identifying individuals most at risk
   - Difference between labor and sex trafficking in the context of lodging/food/trucking industries 
   - Employees' roles in reporting and responding to trafficking

6. Employers can use their own program or a third-party one that meets these minimum requirements.

7. The Department of Human Services is required to develop its own approved curriculum by July 1, 2020 if an employer chooses not to use their own program.

8. There are new penalties for non-compliance:
   - Local government units can issue notice and enforce compliance
   - Violations not corrected within the cure period allow the Attorney General or State's Attorney to sue the employer
   - Maximum fine of $1,500 per offense

9. The law takes effect January 1, 2026.

The main goal is to increase awareness about human trafficking among employees in hospitality and transportation industries who interact with the public regularly.
