Based on the provided information, here are some key points about human trafficking training requirements and resources for commercial lodging establishments in Texas:

1. Requirement:
   - Passed by House Bill 390 (87th Regular Session)
   - Commercial lodging establishments with 10 or more rooms must provide annual human trafficking training to all new employees within 90 days of hire
   - All employees must receive this training annually

2. Training Content:
   - Must cover identifying and responding to human trafficking
   - Discusses the role of commercial lodging in addressing sex trafficking
   - Explains how human trafficking occurs in the hospitality industry

3. Signage Requirement:
   - Commercial lodging establishments must display specific signage related to human trafficking

4. Reporting Information:
   - Training must include contact information for reporting human trafficking incidents (National Human Trafficking Hotline, local law enforcement)
   - Must direct employees to call 911 in emergencies and other resources

5. Certification:
   - Providers must issue certificates of completion
   - Employers must retain copies of these certificates

6. Training Approvals:
   - Office of the Attorney General (OAG) approves training videos
   - Not all approved trainings are mandatory, but encouraged for all lodging establishments regardless of size

7. Approved Training Resources:
   - The OAG provides a list of approved human trafficking training videos and organizations offering them
   - Various organizations offer online and in-person training sessions at different costs

8. Accessibility:
   - Some trainings are free
   - Training materials are available in English and Spanish

9. Reporting Requirements:
   - Employers must maintain records showing compliance with Section 114.0051 of the Texas Business and Commerce Code
   - These records must be available for review by OAG, local, state, and federal law enforcement upon request

Overall, these requirements aim to increase awareness and response capabilities among hospitality industry workers regarding human trafficking issues in commercial lodging establishments.
