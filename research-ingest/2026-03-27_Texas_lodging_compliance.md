The Texas Legislature passed House Bill 390, which requires the Office of the Attorney General (OAG) to approve training for the hospitality industry. The training is aimed at providing workers directly employed by certain commercial lodging establishments with additional opportunities to report human trafficking. This annual training requirement only applies to commercial lodging establishments of 10 or more rooms, but it is encouraged for all lodging establishments in the State of Texas regardless of size.
The OAG has approved several human trafficking training videos that can be accessed online or in-person at a cost. These include:

  * Human Trafficking Hotel Training by Etactics
  * Human Trafficking - Texas by Unbound Now
  * Identifying and Responding to Human Trafficking: Commercial Lodging & Hospitality Industry by Unbound Now
  * Inhospitable to Human Trafficking Training by Businesses Ending Slavery and Trafficking
  * Human Trafficking Training by Extended Stay America
  * Recognize and Respond: Addressing Human Trafficking in the Hospitality Industry by Marriott International
  * Your Role in Preventing Human Trafficking: Recognize the Signs by ECPAT-USA
  * Survivor Services/HT101 by Refugee Services of Texas
  * Human Trafficking Essentials: Texas Hospitality Awareness HB 390 by PBJ Learning
  * Combating Human Trafficking in the Hospitality Industry by Compliance Learning Solutions

It is important to note that all employees must be trained annually, and operators of commercial lodging establishments are required to maintain sufficient records to show compliance with Section 114.0051, Texas Business and Commerce Code. These records must be made available for review and inspection at the request of the OAG, as well as local, state, and federal law enforcement.
