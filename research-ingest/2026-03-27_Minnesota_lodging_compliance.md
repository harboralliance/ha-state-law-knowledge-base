### Summary of Sex Trafficking Prevention Training for Hotels, Motels, and Short-Term Rentals in Minnesota

#### Legal Requirements:
- **Who Must Take the Training:**
  - All owners, managers, and on-site employees at hotels, motels, and short-term rentals must complete the training.
  - Exemptions include employees under 16 (unless they clean rooms), those working exclusively in restaurants providing catering services, and those with no direct contact with guests or guest rooms.

- **Timeline:**
  - New employees must take the training within 90 days of hire.
  - All required staff must retake the training annually.

#### Training Content:
- **Duration:** The training is approximately 45 to 50 minutes long.
- **Format:**
  - A virtual presentation with guided exercises
  - Training Companion Guide (PDF)
  - Manager & Owner Training Toolkit (PDF)
  - Back of House Poster set

#### Key Topics Covered:
- Signs and indicators of sex trafficking
- How to respond if they suspect or identify a victim
- Legal obligations and resources for reporting suspicious activities
- Importance of ongoing awareness campaigns for all employees

#### Display Requirements:
- **Back of House Poster:** Must be displayed in a location where all staff can see it.

#### Additional Compliance:
- Conduct an ongoing awareness campaign for employees.
- Regularly update the training package by November 1 each year.

#### Availability and Resources:
- Training materials are available in English, Spanish, Somali, Hmong.
- Managers and owners can access the training through the MDH Learning Center.
- Employees can complete their training in any available language.
- The law does not charge a fee for the mandatory training package.
- MDH provides the free training package but does not cover additional costs such as printing posters.

#### Contact Information:
For questions or assistance accessing the training, contact health.safeharbor@state.mn.us

### Conclusion
Compliance with the Minnesota Sex Trafficking Prevention Training legislation is crucial for protecting against sex trafficking in hotel, motel, and short-term rental environments. By providing comprehensive training to all required staff and adhering to display requirements and ongoing awareness campaigns, these establishments can play a vital role in preventing sex trafficking and upholding the rights of potential victims.
