### Minnesota Sex Trafficking Prevention Training for Hotel, Motel, and Short-Term Rental Staff

#### Overview
Sex trafficking occurs in various settings, including hotels, motels, and short-term rentals. To prevent this form of exploitation, hotel, motel, and short-term rental staff are required to complete specific training.

#### Legal Requirement
The training is mandatory under Minnesota law (Minnesota Statutes § 169.274).

#### Who Must Complete the Training
- All owners, managers, and on-site employees must complete the required training.
- Exceptions include:
  - Employees under 16 unless they clean rooms.
  - Employees who work exclusively in a restaurant, providing catering services, or both.
  - Employees with no direct contact with guests or guest rooms.

#### Training Requirements
- New employees must take this training within 90 days of hire.
- All required staff must retake the training every year.
- Display the required back-of-house poster in a location where all staff will see it.
- Conduct an ongoing awareness campaign for employees.

#### Training Package Components
The Minnesota Lodging Sex Trafficking Prevention and Response Training Package includes:
- A virtual presentation (video with guided exercises)
- Training Companion Guide (PDF) as a resource for viewing the virtual presentation
- Manager & Owner Training Toolkit (PDF) to comply with the law
- Poster set, including:
  - Back of House (required to display)
  - Housekeeping
  - Restaurant/Bar
  - Front desk/lobby
  - Security

#### Accessing the Training
- **Managers and Owners**: Complete a brief pre-training survey before accessing the training package through the MDH Learning Center.
- **Employees**: Training is available in multiple languages (English, Spanish/Español, Somali, Hmong).

#### FAQs
1. **Do I need to take this training if I already took sex trafficking training elsewhere?**
   - Yes, you must complete the Minnesota-specific training.

2. **Are resorts, bed and breakfasts, vacation shares, short-term rentals required to provide the training?**
   - Only if they hold a hotel license.

3. **Does the law apply to tribal nations?**
   - No, but MDH encourages tribal nations to provide the training.

4. **What languages are available for the training materials?**
   - The package will be offered in Spanish, Hmong, and Somali starting late fall 2023/early 2024.

5. **Does MDH assist with any costs of compliance?**
   - Aside from providing the free training package, MDH does not provide assistance like printing posters.

6. **Do managers/owners and employees have to take the training every year?**
   - Yes, it is an annual requirement tied to licensure. Updates will be provided by November 1 each year.

7. **Can we replace the MDH training with our own materials?**
   - No, it can't be replaced. In limited situations, a part may be replaced but only with prior review and approval by MDH.

8. **If a hotel owner/operator/manager does not have contact with guests, are they required to take the training?**
   - Yes, they are still required to take the training.

9. **Does the law exempt seasonal employees?**
   - The law does not explicitly exempt seasonal employees, but if covered under the license, they must receive the training.

10. **If an employee is temporary and only employed for two months, do they need to complete the training?**
    - Technically no, but MDH encourages all employees.
