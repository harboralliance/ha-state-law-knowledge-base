### Summary

The key requirements and information related to human trafficking awareness training for employees at motels and hotels in California are as follows:

1. **Training Requirement**: Hotel and motel employers must provide at least 20 minutes of classroom or other effective interactive training and education regarding human trafficking awareness to each employee who is likely to interact or come into contact with victims of human trafficking.

2. **Timing for Training**:
   - Employees employed as of July 1, 2019, need this training by January 1, 2020.
   - New employees must receive the same training within six months of their employment in a role that may involve interacting with victims of human trafficking.

3. **Existing Training Compliance**: Employers who have provided this training on or before January 1, 2019, are not required to provide additional training for those employees at that time.

4. **Enforcement**: The requirement is governed by California state law, specifically Senate Bill (SB) 970, which was enacted in 2018 and became effective in January 2020.

5. **Public Awareness**: Employers are also required to post a notice informing the public about human trafficking resources and hotlines.

6. **Resources**: Additional support and resources for employers include model notices available from the California Attorney General’s Office and further information on legal compliance and training options.

### Compliance Timeline

- **Initial Training Requirement**: January 1, 2020 (for employees employed as of July 1, 2019)
- **Training for New Employees**: Within six months of employment for roles that may involve interaction with victims of human trafficking.

### Enforcement and Penalties

Enforcement of this requirement is likely to be handled through the California Department of Labor Standards Enforcement (DLSE) and other relevant state agencies. Non-compliance could lead to penalties or fines, as well as potential legal actions against employers who fail to comply with the training requirements.

### Support for Employers

Employers can seek additional resources from the California Attorney General’s Office, including model notices and further guidance on how to comply with the law effectively. This support is crucial in ensuring that employees receive appropriate training without excessive burden on businesses.
