### Analysis and Summary

The provided sources collectively outline a comprehensive policy framework regarding human trafficking awareness training for employees in hotels and motels, specifically in California.

#### 1. **State Law Requirement (SB 970)**
   - **Effective Date**: January 1, 2020.
   - **Training Requirements**: 
     - Hotel and motel employers must provide at least 20 minutes of classroom or other effective interactive training regarding human trafficking awareness to employees who are likely to interact with or come into contact with victims of human trafficking.
     - This training must be provided to all such employees employed as of July 1, 2019, as well as to new employees within six months of their employment in the role.

#### 2. **Training Content**
   - The content of the training is not explicitly detailed in the provided sources but is expected to cover essential knowledge regarding human trafficking, its forms, indicators, and how to respond appropriately if encountered or reported.

#### 3. **Penalties for Non-Compliance**
   - There is no specific mention of penalties for non-compliance in the provided sources. However, failure to comply with such requirements could result in legal penalties as per general employment laws.

#### 4. **Public Notification Requirement (Senate Bill 1193)**
   - Businesses required to post notices informing the public and victims about human trafficking hotline numbers.
   - Hotels, motels, and bed and breakfasts are included in this requirement.

#### 5. **Model Notice**
   - A model notice is available on the California Department of Justice website for download. The notice must be posted in English and Spanish.
   - For businesses located in specific counties that fall under the language assistance provisions of the federal Voting Rights Act, a third language posting may be required.

#### 6. **Resources for Victims**
   - Victims can call various hotlines or text messages for services and support:
     - National Human Trafficking Resource Center: (888) 373-7888
     - California Coalition to Abolish Slavery and Trafficking (CAST): (1-888-KEY-2FRE(EDOM)) or (1-888-539-2373)

#### Conclusion
The policy requires hotels and motels in California to provide mandatory human trafficking awareness training to their employees, ensuring they are equipped with the knowledge to recognize and respond appropriately if they encounter victims of human trafficking. The state also mandates that these businesses post public notices informing about human trafficking hotlines, enhancing community awareness and support for victims. Compliance with these requirements is crucial to protect vulnerable individuals and uphold California's commitment to combating human trafficking.
