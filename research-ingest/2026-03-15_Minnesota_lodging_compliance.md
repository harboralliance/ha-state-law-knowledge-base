The key points regarding sex trafficking prevention training for hotels, motels, and short-term rentals in Minnesota are as follows:

### Legal Requirements:
- **Training Requirement**: Owners, managers, and on-site employees must complete a required training program annually.
- **New Employees**: New hires must complete the training within 90 days of their start date.
- **Exceptions**: The law provides some exceptions (e.g., employees under 16 who clean rooms, restaurant staff).

### Training Package:
- **Duration**: Typically takes 45 to 50 minutes to complete.
- **Materials**:
  - Virtual presentation with guided exercises
  - Training Companion Guide (PDF)
  - Manager & Owner Training Toolkit (PDF)
  - Back of House Poster set

### Display and Awareness Campaigns:
- **Back of House Poster**: Required to be displayed in a visible location.
- **Awareness Campaign**: Managers must conduct an ongoing awareness campaign for all employees.

### Accessibility and Language Options:
- **English**: Employee Training
- **Spanish/Español**: Employee Training
- **Somali**: Employee Training
- **Hmong**: Employee Training

### Implementation:
- **Managers/Owners**: Access via MDH Learning Center, completing a pre-training survey.
- **Employees**: Access in their preferred language.

### Key Points for Compliance:
- **Training Annuality**: The training must be completed annually by all required staff.
- **Display Poster**: The back of house poster must remain displayed at all times.
- **Awareness Campaigns**: Ongoing campaigns are necessary to ensure full awareness among employees.

This comprehensive approach ensures that hotels, motels, and short-term rentals in Minnesota play a crucial role in preventing sex trafficking by providing education and clear guidelines for staff.
