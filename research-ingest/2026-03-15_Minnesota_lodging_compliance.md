### Overview of Minnesota's Sex Trafficking Prevention Training Requirement

#### Legal Mandate:
Under Minnesota law, hotels, motels, and short-term rentals are required to provide training on sex trafficking prevention and response to their employees.

#### Key Requirements:
1. **Compliance Timeline:**
   - All hotel, motel, and short-term rental owners, managers, and on-site employees must complete the required training.
   - New hires must complete the training within 90 days of employment.
   - All required staff must retake the training annually.

2. **Training Content:**
   - The training includes a virtual presentation, a Training Companion Guide (PDF), a Manager & Owner Training Toolkit (PDF), and a poster set that must be displayed in various areas of the establishment.

3. **Display of Poster:**
   - Hotels, motels, and short-term rentals are required to display the back of house poster prominently.

4. **Awareness Campaign:**
   - Regular awareness campaigns for employees on recognizing and responding to signs of sex trafficking are mandated.

#### Exceptions:
- Employees under 16 who clean rooms.
- Employees who work exclusively in a restaurant or providing catering services without direct contact with guests or guest rooms.
- Owners, operators, and managers must also complete the training as they have responsibility for ensuring compliance.

#### Training Accessibility:
- The Minnesota Department of Health (MDH) offers the training package in multiple languages, including Spanish, Somali, and Hmong.

### Additional Resources:

1. **Minnesota Human Trafficking Prevention and Response Network (MNHTPRN):**
   - Provides a platform for learning more about human trafficking prevention and response efforts across the state.
   - Offers resources for employers, employees, and community members.

2. **MDH Learning Center:**
   - Managers and owners can access the training through this center, where they will complete a pre-training survey before accessing the package.

3. **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs):**
   - MDH provides detailed FAQs to help clarify requirements and address specific questions about the training mandate.

### Contact Information:

### Conclusion:
The sex trafficking prevention training requirement in Minnesota is designed to ensure that hotel, motel, and short-term rental staff are equipped with the knowledge to recognize and respond effectively to cases of sex trafficking. Compliance with this law is crucial for protecting employees and preventing exploitation within these establishments.
