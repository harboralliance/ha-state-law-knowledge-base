### Overview of Sex Trafficking Prevention Training for Hotels, Motels, and Short-Term Rentals in Minnesota

#### Legal Requirement:
The training is mandated by **Minnesota law** and applies to all owners, managers, and on-site employees at hotels, motels, and short-term rentals.

#### Training Requirements:
1. **Completion Time**: 45-50 minutes.
2. **Frequency of Training**: 
   - New employees must complete the training within 90 days of hire.
   - All required staff must retake the training every year.
3. **Display of Poster**: The back-of-house poster is required and must be displayed in a location all staff can see.
4. **Ongoing Awareness Campaign**: Hotels, motels, and short-term rentals are encouraged to conduct an ongoing awareness campaign for employees.

#### Training Package:
The Minnesota Lodging Sex Trafficking Prevention and Response Training Package includes:
- Virtual Presentation (Video with guided exercises)
- Training Companion Guide (PDF) as a resource
- Manager & Owner Training Toolkit (PDF) for compliance with the law
- Poster set: 
  - Back of House (required to display)
  - Housekeeping
  - Restaurant/Bar
  - Front desk/lobby
  - Security

#### Exceptions:
- Employees under 16 unless they clean rooms.
- Employees who work exclusively in a restaurant, providing catering services, or both.
- Employees with no direct contact with guests or guest rooms.

#### Access to Training:
- **Managers and Owners**: Access through the MDH Learning Center.
- **Employee Training**: Available in English, Spanish, Somali, and Hmong.

#### Additional Information:
- The training is not mandatory for resorts, bed and breakfasts, vacation shares, short-term rentals unless they hold a hotel license.
- MDH encourages tribal nations to provide the training using MDH materials.
- There are no fees for the mandatory training package, but licensees are responsible for printing posters.
- Training materials will be offered in additional languages (Spanish, Hmong, Somali) starting late fall 2023/early 2024.

#### Contact Information:
For questions or assistance accessing the training, contact **health.safeharbor@state.mn.us**.

This comprehensive overview ensures that all relevant parties are aware of their responsibilities under Minnesota law and understand how to properly implement sex trafficking prevention training.
