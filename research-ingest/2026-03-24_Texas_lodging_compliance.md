**Human Trafficking Training for Commercial Lodging Establishments in Texas**

The Texas Legislature passed House Bill 390 (87th Regular Session), which requires the Office of the Attorney General (OAG) to approve training for the hospitality industry. The goal is to provide additional opportunities for human trafficking to be reported to law enforcement by requiring human trafficking training for workers directly employed by certain commercial lodging establishments.

### Requirements
- **Commercial Lodging Establishments**: Only those with 10 or more rooms are required, but it's encouraged for all lodging establishments regardless of size.
- **Annual Training**: All employees must complete annual training.
- **Certificate of Completion**: Every employee who completes the training must receive a certificate that includes the date and title of the course.

### Training Content
The training must cover:
1. How human trafficking takes place in the hospitality industry.
2. Commercial lodging's role in addressing sex trafficking.
3. Identification of hotel contract workers vulnerable to labor trafficking.
4. The role of staff in reporting and responding to human trafficking, including guidance on how to respond to any victims asking for assistance.
5. Contact information for appropriate entities to report human trafficking.
6. Information on the required human trafficking signage.
