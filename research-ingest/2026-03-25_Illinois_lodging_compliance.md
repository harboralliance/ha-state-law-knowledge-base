Based on the information provided in the legislation, here are the key points regarding human trafficking training requirements for lodging establishments:

1. Training Requirement: 
- Employers (lodging, restaurant or truck stop operators) must provide their employees with human trafficking recognition training.
- Training must be completed within 6 months of starting employment in that role.
- Employees must complete retraining every 2 years if still employed.

2. Content Requirements:
The training must include:
- Definition of human trafficking and commercial exploitation of children
- How to identify individuals at risk for trafficking 
- Differences between labor vs sex trafficking related to lodging/restaurants/truck stops  
- Role of employees in reporting and responding to trafficking

3. Curriculum Source:
Employers can use their own program, a third-party program, or the state-developed curriculum.

4. Penalties:
Non-compliance can result in enforcement by local government/LEA.
Failure to correct violations within 30 days could lead to a civil penalty of up to $1500 per violation by the Attorney General or State's Attorney.

5. Effective Date: 
The law takes effect January 1, 2026.

So in summary, lodging operators need to provide annual human trafficking training for employees within 6 months of starting employment in that role. The content and enforcement mechanisms are outlined in detail in this legislation.
