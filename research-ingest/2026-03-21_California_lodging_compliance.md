### Key Points from Sources:

1. **SB 970 Requirement**:
   - Effective January 1, 2020, hotels and motels in California must provide at least 20 minutes of training on human trafficking awareness to employees who are likely to interact with victims.
   - The training is required for both existing employees (hired on or before July 1, 2019) and new hires within six months of their employment.
   - Employers must also provide such training to any current employees hired after July 1, 2019, but only if they were not provided with the initial training by their previous employer.

2. **Training Content**:
   - The training should include information on identifying potential victims of human trafficking and how to respond appropriately.
   - Employers are encouraged to provide additional resources or certifications for employees who complete the required training.

3. **Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
   - Failure to comply with the training requirement can result in fines, civil penalties, and other legal actions as per California law (Civil Code § 52.7).

4. **Model Notice**:
   - The Attorney General’s Office provides a model notice that businesses are required to post, which includes contact information for the National Human Trafficking Hotline.
   - This notice must be posted in English and Spanish, and in additional languages if required by federal law.

5. **Public Awareness**:
   - Posting requirements ensure that the public is informed about available resources and support services for victims of human trafficking.

6. **Role of the Attorney General's Office**:
   - The Attorney General’s Office plays a critical role in enforcing California’s human trafficking laws, providing training, and supporting victims.
   - They offer resources like model notices and hotlines to assist businesses in compliance with the law and to help victims.

7. **Enforcement**:
   - The Department of Justice's Victims’ Services Unit can be contacted for assistance in responding to human trafficking incidents and enforcing compliance with state laws.

### Summary:
The California legislature has mandated that hotels and motels provide training on human trafficking awareness to their employees. This requirement helps protect vulnerable individuals, promote public safety, and aligns with the Attorney General’s efforts to combat human trafficking. Non-compliance can lead to legal penalties, and businesses must ensure compliance through appropriate postings and employee training.

### Additional Considerations:
- **Training Effectiveness**: The effectiveness of the training will depend on how comprehensively it is delivered and whether employees retain and apply the learned information.
- **Ongoing Support**: Continuous support and resources are needed for employees to effectively identify and assist potential victims of human trafficking.
- **Legal Compliance**: Businesses should review their compliance measures regularly and ensure they meet all legal requirements related to human trafficking.

This summary provides a clear overview of the legal requirements, roles involved, and the importance of compliance in combating human trafficking.
