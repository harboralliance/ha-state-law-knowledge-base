## Summary
Connecticut provides a variety of trainings and legislation to combat human trafficking, including sex and labor trafficking. Training is required for various service providers such as DCF staff, foster parents, schools, law enforcement, hospital staff, hospitality industry professionals, and youth. Legislation includes mandatory training for hotels, motels, and lodging businesses, expanding protections for victims, and creating multidisciplinary teams to address cases.

## Extracted Requirements
- **Applicable industry:** DCF staff, foster parents, schools, law enforcement, hospital staff, hospitality industry professionals, youth.
- **Training required:** Introduction to Child Trafficking in Connecticut, Introduction to Human Trafficking in Connecticut for Law Enforcement, Introduction to Labor Trafficking in Connecticut, etc.
- **Signage required:** Not specified in the provided sources.
- **Reporting required:** Yes, mandatory data reporting is required.
- **Enforcement agency:** Department of Children and Families (DCF), law enforcement agencies, hotels, motels, inns, similar lodging.
- **Penalties:** Prostitution is a class C felony, commercial sexual exploitation of a minor is a class C felony, etc.

## Summary of Legislation
Connecticut's laws addressing human trafficking include:
- Expanding the crime of human trafficking to minors under 18.
- Providing services and training for victims.
- Creating multidisciplinary teams to work with cases.
- Ongoing monitoring efforts.
- Mandatory data reporting.
- A statewide oversight and monitoring body.

## Conclusion
Connecticut has comprehensive measures in place to combat human trafficking, including mandatory trainings, legislation expanding protections for victims, and multidisciplinary team collaborations. These efforts reflect a proactive approach to preventing and addressing this serious issue within the state.
