### Summary of Sex Trafficking Prevention Training for Hotels, Motels, and Short-Term Rentals in Minnesota

#### Legal Requirement:
Hotels, motels, and short-term rentals are required to comply with Minnesota law by providing training to owners, managers, and on-site employees. This requirement is enforced under the **Minnesota Sex Trafficking Prevention Training Act**.

#### Key Requirements for Compliance:
1. **Training Completion:**
   - All new employees must complete the training within 90 days of hire.
   - All required staff must retake the training every year.

2. **Displaying the Poster:**
   - Owners and managers are required to display a specific poster in back-of-house areas such as housekeeping, restaurant/bar, front desk/lobby, and security.

3. **Ongoing Awareness Campaign:**
   - Hotels, motels, and short-term rentals must conduct an ongoing awareness campaign for employees.

#### Training Content:
The training covers signs of sex trafficking, recognizing potential victims, and steps to take in response to suspected cases. It is available in multiple languages, including English, Spanish, Somali, and Hmong.

#### Exceptions to Training Requirements:
- Employees under 16 who do not work in housekeeping.
- Employees who work exclusively in a restaurant providing catering services without direct contact with guests or guest rooms.

#### Training Delivery:
- The training can be accessed through the Minnesota Department of Health (MDH) Learning Center.
- Managers and owners complete a pre-training survey before accessing the package.
- Employee training is available in various languages, including English, Spanish, Somali, and Hmong.

#### Additional Resources:
- **Minnesota Human Trafficking Prevention and Response Network (MNHTPRN):** A resource network aimed at preventing and responding to human trafficking in Minnesota.
- **Frequently Asked Questions:** The MDH provides a list of FAQs to help businesses understand their obligations and address common concerns related to the training requirement.

#### Compliance:
Licensees are required to pay for any costs associated with compliance, such as printing posters. The MDH does not provide additional financial assistance beyond offering the free training package.

### Conclusion
The Minnesota Sex Trafficking Prevention Training Act requires hotels, motels, and short-term rentals to provide comprehensive training to their staff annually. This includes mandatory attendance by new employees within 90 days of hire, display of a specific poster in designated areas, and ongoing awareness campaigns. Compliance is essential for these businesses to prevent sex trafficking and ensure the safety of their guests.
