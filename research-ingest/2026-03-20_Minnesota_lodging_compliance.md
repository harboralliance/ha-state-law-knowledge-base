**Minnesota Sex Trafficking Prevention Training Overview**

### Legal Requirements

1. **Who Must Take the Training:**
   - All hotel, motel, and short-term rental owners, managers, and on-site employees must complete the required training.
   - Exceptions include:
     - Employees under 16 unless they clean rooms.
     - Employees who work exclusively in a restaurant or providing catering services with no direct contact with guests.

2. **Timing:**
   - New employees must take this training within 90 days of hire.
   - All required staff must retake this training every year.

### Training Requirements

1. **Duration:**
   - The training will take approximately 45 to 50 minutes to complete.

2. **Content:**
   - Virtual presentation with guided exercises
   - Training Companion Guide (PDF) as a resource for viewing the virtual presentation
   - Manager & Owner Training Toolkit (PDF) to comply with the law

3. **Display Requirements:**
   - Back of House poster must be displayed in a location all staff will see it.

4. **Ongoing Awareness Campaign:**
   - Managers and owners must conduct an ongoing awareness campaign for employees.

### Training Package

The training package is required for all hotels, motels, and short-term rentals in Minnesota with very few exceptions. The package includes:

- A virtual presentation
- Training Companion Guide (PDF)
- Manager & Owner Training Toolkit (PDF)
- Back of House poster set
  - Housekeeping
  - Restaurant/Bar
  - Front desk/lobby
  - Security

### Access and Support

1. **Access:**
   - Managers and owners can access the training through MDH Learning Center.
   - Employees can access the employee training in multiple languages (English, Spanish, Somali, Hmong).

2. **Support:**
   - MDH does not charge a fee for the mandatory training package.
   - They do not provide assistance with printing posters. Licensees are required to pay the costs of compliance.
   - If you need materials in other languages, contact health.safeharbor@state.mn.us.

### Compliance

1. **Annual Requirement:**
   - Training must be completed annually.
   - Updates will be provided by November 1 of each year.

2. **Replacement of MDH Materials:**
   - Replacement with own training materials is not allowed except in limited situations approved by MDH.

3. **Exemptions for Owners and Managers:**
   - Exemptions in the laws for employees who do not have contact with guests do not apply to owners, operators, and managers.

### Contact

For any questions or assistance accessing the training, please contact health.safeharbor@state.mn.us.

---

This comprehensive overview ensures that hotel, motel, and short-term rental staff are well-equipped to identify and respond to signs of sex trafficking, thereby helping prevent these tragedies.
