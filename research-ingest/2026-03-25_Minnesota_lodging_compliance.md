### Overview of Sex Trafficking Prevention Training for Hotels, Motels, and Short-Term Rentals

#### Purpose
The purpose of this training package is to educate hotel, motel, and short-term rental staff on how to identify potential signs of sex trafficking and respond appropriately. This training aims to help these establishments contribute to the prevention and response to sex trafficking in Minnesota.

### Training Requirements
1. **Who Must Take the Training:**
   - All owners, managers, and on-site employees must complete the required training.
   - New employees must take this training within 90 days of hire.
   - All required staff must retake this training every year.

2. **Training Content:**
   - The training will take approximately 45 to 50 minutes to complete.
   - It includes a virtual presentation with guided exercises, a Training Companion Guide (PDF), and the Manager & Owner Training Toolkit (PDF).

3. **Post-Training Actions:**
   - Display the required back-of-house poster in a location all staff will see it.
   - Conduct an ongoing awareness campaign for employees.

### Key Points of the Training

1. **Recognition of Sex Trafficking:**
   - The training covers recognizing indicators that may indicate human trafficking, such as unusual travel patterns, lack of personal items, and signs of emotional distress or fear.
   
2. **Reporting Mechanisms:**
   - Staff are trained on how to report suspected cases of sex trafficking through appropriate channels, including local law enforcement.

3. **Employee Awareness Campaign:**
   - An ongoing awareness campaign for employees is a critical part of the training, emphasizing the importance of vigilance and adherence to established protocols.

4. **Training Materials Availability:**
   - Training materials are available in multiple languages, including English, Spanish, Somali, and Hmong, starting late fall 2023/early 2024. If additional language materials are needed, they can be requested by contacting the MDH Safe Harbor Program at `health.safeharbor@state.mn.us`.

### Compliance and Costs
1. **Compliance:**
   - The training is mandatory under Minnesota law.
   - Exceptions exist for certain employees (e.g., those under 16 unless cleaning rooms, those working exclusively in restaurants or bars), but these exceptions do not apply to owners, managers, and operators.

2. **Costs:**
   - MDH provides the training package at no cost to licensees.
   - However, the law requires that licensees pay for the costs of compliance, including printing posters.

### Next Steps
1. **Completion of Training:**
   - After completing the training, managers and owners must provide a roster of all employees who completed the training along with the date of completion. This roster should be kept on file for inspections.

2. **Contact Information:**
   - For any questions regarding the training or compliance requirements, contact the MDH Safe Harbor Program at `health.safeharbor@state.mn.us`.

### Conclusion
The Minnesota Lodging Sex Trafficking Prevention and Response Training is a critical tool for hotel, motel, and short-term rental staff to help prevent and respond to sex trafficking. By completing this training and adhering to its requirements, facilities can play a significant role in protecting vulnerable individuals in their communities.
