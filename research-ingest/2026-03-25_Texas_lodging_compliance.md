### Overview of Human Trafficking Training for Commercial Lodging in Texas

The Texas Legislature passed House Bill 390 (87th Regular Session) to require annual human trafficking training for employees at commercial lodging establishments with ten or more rooms. This legislation aims to enhance reporting and response mechanisms for human trafficking within the hospitality industry, ensuring that workers are better equipped to identify potential victims and take appropriate actions.

### Key Requirements

1. **Annual Training Requirement**: Commercial lodging establishments must provide annual human trafficking training to all employees.
2. **Training Content**: The training must cover:
   - Identification of human trafficking indicators
   - Reporting procedures for suspected cases
   - Legal protections for those reporting trafficking
   - Role in addressing sex and labor trafficking in the hospitality industry

3. **Certificate of Completion**: Employees must receive a certificate of completion, which includes the date and title of the course. These certificates must be retained by the establishment.

4. **Contact Information**: Training materials must provide contact information for appropriate entities to report human trafficking, including:
   - National Human Trafficking Hotline
   - Local law enforcement agencies
   - Attorney General's designated hotline

5. **Training Provider Approval**: Only approved training providers can deliver the required training courses.
